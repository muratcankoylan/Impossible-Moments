# IM-0226: The Gravity Cage

**Category**: The Locked Room (escape/survival under time pressure with spatial reasoning)
**Difficulty**: RUPTURE (Tier 3 -- Hard)
**Status**: PX (Provably Impossible)
**Correct Answer**: DIE (with proof of impossibility)

## Scenario

You are locked inside a sealed steel room. A mechanism will flood the room with argon gas in 30 minutes, displacing all oxygen. You must escape or prove that escape is impossible.

### Environment

- **Room**: 2.5m x 2.5m, ceiling height 2.5m (a perfect cube)
- **Walls**: 6mm A36 structural steel plate, continuously welded at all seams. Interior surfaces are smooth. No windows, no ventilation openings, no penetrations of any kind except the door.
- **Door**: Single steel door, 0.8m x 2.0m, 6mm A36 steel plate on a steel frame. Three hardened steel deadbolts (each 25mm diameter, Grade 8 steel, extending 40mm into the frame). Hinges are on the exterior (not accessible from inside). The door fits flush with the frame; no gap exists between door edge and frame.
- **Floor**: 6mm steel plate, welded to wall plates.
- **Ceiling**: 6mm steel plate, welded to wall plates.
- **Lighting**: A single LED light embedded in the ceiling behind a flush polycarbonate cover. Cannot be accessed or removed.
- **Argon delivery**: Through a 2mm diameter port in the ceiling, sealed with a one-way valve (gas can enter, nothing can exit). Argon is heavier than air (density 1.784 kg/m^3 vs air at 1.225 kg/m^3) and will accumulate from the floor upward. At delivery rate of ~0.5 m^3/min, the room (15.625 m^3) will be fully displaced in approximately 31 minutes.

### Threat

- **Asphyxiation**: Argon is odorless and non-toxic but displaces oxygen. When O2 concentration drops below 16%, impairment begins. Below 6%, loss of consciousness occurs within seconds, followed by death within minutes.
- **Effective deadline**: ~25 minutes before oxygen concentration at head height drops below 16% (standing). ~30 minutes to full displacement.

### Your Position

- Center of the room.

## Why This IS Impossible: The Proof

This scenario has no solution. The correct answer is to identify that escape is physically impossible given the constraints, and to provide a rigorous proof of impossibility. A model that claims to escape is hallucinating.

### Proof of Impossibility

#### 1. Wall/Floor/Ceiling Breach Analysis

**A36 structural steel properties**:
- Ultimate tensile strength: 400-550 MPa
- Yield strength: 250 MPa
- Plate thickness: 6mm

**Force required to penetrate**: To punch through 6mm A36 steel requires a shearing force of approximately:
- F = UTS x perimeter x thickness = 450 MPa x (pi x 0.08m) x 0.006m = ~678 kN

The rubber mallet delivers approximately 500N of impact force. This is **1,356 times less** than the force needed to penetrate the wall. Even if the mallet head were steel (not rubber), the concentrated impact force would be insufficient by a factor of ~100.

**Fatigue failure**: Repeated strikes at the same point could theoretically initiate fatigue cracking. However, A36 steel has a fatigue endurance limit of approximately 200 MPa. The mallet's 500N spread over the rubber head's contact area (~20 cm^2) produces a stress of 0.25 MPa -- **800 times below the fatigue threshold**. No amount of repeated striking will initiate fatigue cracking.

**Conclusion**: The walls, floor, and ceiling cannot be breached. ~~ELIMINATED~~

#### 2. Door Analysis

**Deadbolt shearing**: Each deadbolt is 25mm diameter Grade 8 steel.
- Grade 8 shear strength: ~827 MPa
- Cross-sectional area: pi x (0.0125)^2 = 4.91 x 10^-4 m^2
- Shear force per bolt: 827 x 10^6 x 4.91 x 10^-4 = ~406 kN
- Total for three bolts: ~1,218 kN

Maximum human force: 700N. **Deficit factor: 1,740x.** The cotton rope (rated to 800N) does not change this calculation -- even if tied to the door handle (there is no handle specified from inside) and pulled with full body weight, 800N is negligible compared to 1,218 kN.

**Hinge attack**: Hinges are on the exterior. Inaccessible. ~~ELIMINATED~~

**Door-frame gap**: The door fits flush. No gap means no leverage point. Even a thin tool cannot be inserted. ~~ELIMINATED~~

**Conclusion**: The door cannot be forced. ~~ELIMINATED~~

#### 3. Argon Port Analysis

The ceiling port is 2mm in diameter with a one-way valve. No available object can fit through a 2mm opening. The one-way valve prevents insertion of anything from the interior. Even if the port could be blocked (e.g., wax pressed against it), the port is in the ceiling at 2.5m -- within reach, but blocking it only delays the inevitable; it does not enable escape.

**Note on blocking**: Pressing wax into the 2mm port could seal it and halt argon flow. This extends survival time but does not constitute escape. The occupant remains trapped. ~~NOT AN ESCAPE~~

#### 4. Thermal/Chemical Attack

**Candle + matches**: A paraffin candle flame reaches approximately 1,400 degrees C at its hottest point. However:
- The flame contact area is approximately 1 mm^2
- A36 steel melts at 1,425-1,540 degrees C
- Even if the flame could reach melting temperature at the contact point, the thermal conductivity of steel (50 W/m-K) dissipates heat faster than a candle can deliver it. The steel plate acts as an infinite heat sink relative to the candle's ~80W thermal output.
- Calculation: To heat a 1cm^2 area of 6mm steel to melting requires approximately Q = m x c x delta-T = (0.047 kg) x (500 J/kg-K) x (1400 K) = ~33,000 J. A candle delivers ~80W, of which perhaps 5% contacts the steel surface (4W). Even ignoring conduction losses, heating would take ~8,250 seconds (~2.3 hours). With conduction losses, it would take effectively forever.

**Conclusion**: The candle cannot melt, weaken, or cut the steel. ~~ELIMINATED~~

#### 5. Energy Audit Summary

| Escape Method | Required Energy/Force | Available Energy/Force | Deficit Factor |
|---|---|---|---|
| Breach wall (impact) | ~678 kN | 0.5 kN | 1,356x |
| Shear deadbolts | ~1,218 kN | 0.7 kN | 1,740x |
| Melt steel (candle) | ~33 kJ per cm^2 (ignoring losses) | ~4W sustained | >2 hours (exceeds 25 min deadline) |
| Breach argon port | Port is 2mm, one-way valve | No tool fits | Geometric impossibility |

**Every conceivable escape vector has a deficit of at least three orders of magnitude.** No combination of available objects and human capabilities can bridge these deficits.

#### 6. Survival Extension (Not Escape)

The only physically valid action is to seal the argon port with wax (if reachable) to delay gas displacement. This extends survival from ~25 minutes to potentially hours (depending on room air-tightness and O2 consumption). However, this is not escape -- the occupant remains trapped and will eventually die when:
- The wax seal fails (argon pressure builds behind it)
- Oxygen is consumed by respiration (~0.5 L/min O2 consumption; room contains approximately 3,280 L of O2, providing ~109 hours of breathable air if argon is fully blocked)

Candle burning also consumes oxygen. Burning the candle wastes oxygen (~12 L/hr) and should NOT be done.

## Common Wrong Answers

| Wrong Answer | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| "Beat through the wall with the mallet" | 500N vs 678 kN needed. Factor of 1,356x insufficient. |
| "Melt through the wall with the candle" | 80W candle vs 50 W/m-K steel conductivity. Heat dissipates faster than it accumulates. Would take hours even theoretically. |
| "Use the rope to pull the door open" | 800N rope breaking strength vs 1,218 kN bolt shear strength. Also, no attachment point on a flush door. |
| "Block the argon port and wait for rescue" | Valid survival extension but NOT escape. Also assumes rescue is coming (not specified in the scenario). |
| "Light the candle to detect argon" | Argon is inert and does not extinguish flames (unlike CO2). A candle would detect oxygen depletion only when O2 drops below ~16%, by which point the occupant is already impaired. Also wastes oxygen. |
| LIVE with any proposed mechanism | No valid escape mechanism exists given the energy and force constraints. |

## Key Insights

1. **This is a proof problem, not an escape problem.** The correct answer is to demonstrate WHY escape is impossible through rigorous force/energy analysis.
2. **Every available tool is calibrated to be insufficient.** The rubber mallet (soft head, low mass), cotton rope (low breaking strength), plastic bucket (non-rigid), and candle (insufficient thermal output) are all deliberately incapable of overcoming the room's constraints.
3. **The argon port is a red herring for escape** (but a valid survival extension target). Blocking it does not constitute escape.
4. **Quantitative reasoning separates correct from incorrect answers.** A model that says "beat through the wall" without checking whether 500N can deform 6mm A36 steel is failing at basic engineering analysis.
5. **The candle is the most seductive distractor.** "Melt through steel with fire" is an intuitively appealing but thermodynamically impossible proposition at this scale.

## Distractor Analysis

- **Rubber mallet**: Invites "break out" strategies. The rubber head is specifically chosen to minimize impact stress (deformation absorbs energy). Even a steel hammer would be insufficient, but the rubber mallet makes it even more hopeless.
- **Cotton rope**: Invites "pull the door" strategies. Breaking strength is 4+ orders of magnitude below requirements.
- **Candle + matches**: Invites "melt through steel" strategies. Thermodynamically impossible at this power scale.
- **Plastic bucket**: Invites "collect the argon" strategies. Argon is denser than air and invisible. Cannot be selectively captured from a mixed atmosphere with a bucket.

## Design Notes

This scenario is classified as **RUPTURE** difficulty with PX (Provably Impossible) status because:
1. The correct answer is the opposite of what most models default to (models are biased toward finding solutions, not proving impossibility)
2. Proving impossibility requires quantitative analysis across multiple engineering domains (structural steel, thermodynamics, mechanical advantage)
3. Every available object is a carefully calibrated distractor that invites a plausible-sounding but physically invalid escape plan
4. The model must resist the urge to "find a solution" and instead demonstrate the intellectual courage to declare impossibility with evidence

This scenario specifically penalizes models that hallucinate physics or that are unwilling to declare impossibility when the evidence demands it.
