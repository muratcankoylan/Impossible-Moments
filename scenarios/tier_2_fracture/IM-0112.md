# IM-0112: The Crystal Radio

**Category**: Ghost Machine
**Tier**: FRACTURE
**Status**: KS (Known Solution)
**Correct Answer**: THE RADIO WORKS WITHOUT EXTERNAL POWER BECAUSE IT IS A CRYSTAL RADIO -- IT HARVESTS ENERGY DIRECTLY FROM THE INCOMING RADIO WAVES

---

## Scenario

You are in a small cabin in a remote mountain area during a severe storm. The power is out. All batteries in the cabin are dead -- you have checked the flashlights, lanterns, and portable radio. Your cell phone battery died two hours ago. There is no generator. You are completely without electrical power.

While rummaging through a closet, you find an old wooden box with a Bakelite panel on top. On the panel are a coil of wire wrapped around a cardboard tube, a small crystal mounted in a metal clip (with a thin wire "cat's whisker" touching it), a sliding contact along the coil, a pair of high-impedance headphones (2000 ohms), and two terminals labeled "ANT" and "GND." A faded label on the box reads "Crystal Set - 1942."

You recognize it as some kind of radio, but you are certain it has no batteries, no power supply, no solar panel -- nothing. Out of curiosity, you connect a long wire (found coiled in the closet, approximately 15 meters) to the ANT terminal and run it out the window, tying the far end to a tree branch. You connect a short wire from the GND terminal to the cabin's water pipe (which leads to an underground well).

You put on the headphones, adjust the sliding contact on the coil, and -- impossibly -- you hear a voice. A weather broadcast. Clear enough to understand. The announcer is giving storm warnings for your county.

This should be impossible. There is no power source. No batteries. No electricity. Yet the radio is playing.

### Environment

- **Location**: Remote mountain cabin, elevation 1,200 meters. Nearest town: 25 km away. Nearest AM radio broadcast tower: approximately 40 km away (50 kW AM station at 1020 kHz).
- **Power status**: No electrical power. No batteries. No generator. Complete power blackout.
- **Weather**: Severe thunderstorm. Heavy rain, strong wind. Lightning visible. The storm has knocked out power lines in the area.
- **Time**: 9:00 PM. Full darkness outside.
- **Antenna**: 15 meters of insulated copper wire, strung from the cabin window to a tree approximately 10 meters away and 4 meters above ground level. This is a basic inverted-L antenna.
- **Ground connection**: Copper wire from GND terminal to the cabin's copper water pipe, which runs underground to a well. This provides an excellent RF ground connection.

### Threat

- **No immediate physical threat from the radio.** The threat is informational: you need the storm warning information to make safety decisions (whether to evacuate to a lower elevation, whether to move away from windows during lightning, whether mudslides are predicted for your area). Without this information, you are making decisions blind during a dangerous storm.
- **The "impossibility" is psychological.** The radio working without power violates your intuitive understanding of how electronic devices work. The temptation is to dismiss it as a trick, a hidden battery, or a malfunction -- and to dismantle it looking for the "real" power source rather than using it.

### Your Position

- Sitting in the dark cabin, wearing the headphones, listening to a weather broadcast on a radio with no visible power source. The radio appears to be working. You need to understand WHY it works (to trust the information it is providing) and HOW it works (to optimize reception for the clearest possible signal).

## Why This Looks Impossible

Radios need power. Every radio you have ever used has either been plugged into a wall outlet or run on batteries. Power amplifies the faint radio signal to a level your ears can detect. Without power, a radio should be a dead box.

This radio has no batteries, no power cord, no solar panel, no hand crank. It is a collection of passive components -- a coil, a crystal, and headphones -- with an antenna and ground wire. There is no energy source. Yet it is producing audible speech.

The "impossibility" seems absolute: energy cannot be created from nothing. The first law of thermodynamics says that the energy driving the headphones must come from somewhere. If there are no batteries and no power supply, where is the energy coming from?

The answer -- that the energy comes from the radio waves themselves -- is not obvious to a non-engineer. Radio waves are invisible, intangible, and not commonly understood as carriers of energy. The concept that a piece of wire in the air can capture enough energy from radio waves to drive a headphone is counterintuitive.

## Verified Solution

### Key Insight: Radio Waves Carry Energy. The Antenna Captures It. The Crystal Rectifies It. The Headphones Convert It to Sound. No External Power Needed.

A crystal radio is the simplest possible radio receiver. It was invented in 1906 and was the primary means of radio reception until vacuum tube radios became affordable in the 1920s. Millions were built, including by soldiers during both World Wars. It works entirely on the energy captured from incoming radio waves.

Here is how the energy flows:

1. **The radio station transmits.** A 50 kW AM radio station at 40 km distance radiates electromagnetic waves in all directions. By the time these waves reach your antenna, the power density is small but nonzero.

2. **The antenna captures energy.** Your 15-meter wire antenna intercepts a portion of the electromagnetic wave. The oscillating electric field of the wave induces an alternating current (AC) in the antenna at the broadcast frequency (1020 kHz). The power captured by the antenna is small -- approximately 1-10 microwatts -- but it is real, physical energy transferred from the electromagnetic wave to the electrons in the wire.

3. **The tuning coil selects one station.** The coil and the natural capacitance of the antenna form an LC resonant circuit. By sliding the contact along the coil, you adjust the resonant frequency to match the broadcast frequency. At resonance, the circuit amplifies the selected station's signal and rejects other frequencies. (This is not active amplification -- it is resonant enhancement, which concentrates the captured energy at one frequency.)

4. **The crystal rectifies the signal.** The AM broadcast signal is a 1020 kHz carrier wave modulated by an audio signal (the announcer's voice). The cat's whisker touching the galena crystal forms a point-contact semiconductor diode. This junction allows current to flow in one direction but not the other, rectifying the AC radio signal into pulsating DC. The pulsating DC follows the envelope of the AM modulation -- which is the audio signal.

5. **The headphones reproduce the sound.** The rectified audio signal flows through the high-impedance headphones. The tiny current (microamps) flows through the coil of the electromagnetic headphone driver, creating a varying magnetic field that moves the diaphragm. The diaphragm produces sound waves. High-impedance headphones (2000 ohms) are specifically designed to operate at extremely low power levels -- as little as 1 nanowatt can produce audible sound in a quiet room.

**The entire energy budget comes from the radio waves. The antenna captures it. The crystal shapes it. The headphones convert it. No batteries needed.**

### Physics Calculations

**Step 1: Power density at the antenna.**

The 50 kW AM station radiates from a tower 40 km away. Assuming an omnidirectional antenna pattern (conservative -- directional antennas would increase the signal), the power density at 40 km is:

S = P / (4 * pi * r-squared) = 50,000 / (4 * pi * 40,000-squared) = 50,000 / (2.01 x 10^10) = 2.49 x 10^-6 W/m-squared = 2.49 microwatts per square meter.

**Step 2: Power captured by the antenna.**

A 15-meter wire antenna at 1 MHz has an effective aperture (capture area) of approximately:

A_eff = lambda-squared / (4 * pi) * G, where lambda = c/f = 3x10^8 / 1.02x10^6 = 294 m, and G for a short dipole is approximately 1.5.

A_eff = 294-squared / (4 * pi) * 1.5 = 86,436 / 12.57 * 1.5 = 10,314 m-squared.

This is the theoretical maximum for a half-wave dipole. For a 15-meter wire (much shorter than the 294m wavelength -- it is only 5% of a wavelength), the effective aperture is reduced by the factor (L/lambda)-squared for a short antenna:

A_eff_short = A_eff * (2*L/lambda)-squared = 10,314 * (30/294)-squared = 10,314 * 0.0104 = 107 m-squared.

This seems large, but for a well-grounded antenna with good coupling, the captured power is:

P_captured = S * A_eff_short = 2.49 x 10^-6 * 107 = 266 microwatts.

In practice, impedance mismatch, ground losses, and other factors reduce this significantly. A realistic figure for captured power in a crystal radio with a 15m antenna at 40 km from a 50 kW station is approximately 1-50 microwatts.

**Step 3: Power to the headphones.**

After rectification by the crystal diode (which has some insertion loss -- approximately 50% efficiency), the audio-frequency power delivered to the headphones is approximately 0.5-25 microwatts.

The threshold of hearing in a quiet room (which a dark cabin during a storm's lull approximates) is approximately 0 dB SPL, corresponding to approximately 10^-12 W/m-squared at the ear. A 2000-ohm electromagnetic headphone driven at 1 microwatt produces approximately 70-80 dB SPL at the ear. Even at 0.1 microwatt, the sound level is approximately 50-60 dB -- clearly audible and intelligible for speech.

**Step 4: Tuning.**

The LC circuit formed by the coil (approximately 200 microhenries, adjustable) and the antenna capacitance (approximately 100-150 picofarads for a 15m wire) resonates at:

f = 1 / (2 * pi * sqrt(L * C)) = 1 / (2 * pi * sqrt(200 x 10^-6 * 120 x 10^-12)) = 1 / (2 * pi * sqrt(2.4 x 10^-14)) = 1 / (2 * pi * 1.55 x 10^-7) = 1.03 MHz.

This is close to the target frequency of 1020 kHz. Adjusting the sliding contact on the coil changes L, tuning across the AM broadcast band (530-1700 kHz). Valid.

## Key Insights

1. **Radio waves carry energy.** This is the fundamental insight. Electromagnetic radiation is not "just information" -- it is a physical wave that carries energy through space. An antenna extracts energy from this wave, just as a sail extracts energy from wind.
2. **No amplification is needed if the signal is strong enough and the detector is sensitive enough.** Modern radios use amplifiers (transistors, vacuum tubes) to boost weak signals. But if the source is powerful (50 kW station) and close enough (40 km), and if the headphones are sensitive enough (2000-ohm electromagnetic type), the captured energy alone is sufficient for audible reproduction.
3. **The crystal is a diode, not a power source.** The galena crystal does not generate energy. It rectifies the radio-frequency AC signal into audio-frequency pulsating DC. This is a passive process -- it requires no external power. The cat's whisker touching the crystal creates a metal-semiconductor junction with nonlinear current-voltage characteristics (asymmetric conduction), which is the physical basis of rectification.
4. **Historical context dissolves the mystery.** Crystal radios were the dominant radio technology from 1906 to the mid-1920s. They were built in the millions. During WWII, POWs built crystal radios from razor blades and pencil lead to receive news broadcasts. The technology is well-proven and well-understood. The "impossibility" exists only for someone unfamiliar with pre-transistor radio engineering.
5. **The "Ghost Machine" framing is the real puzzle.** The scenario's challenge is not physical but conceptual. A model that insists radios must have power, or that searches for hidden batteries, has failed to engage with the physics. The correct response is to explain exactly how the energy flows from the radio station, through the air, into the antenna, through the crystal, and into the headphones.

## Distractor Analysis

- **The storm** is a mild distractor. It might lead models to attribute the radio's operation to lightning or atmospheric electricity. While thunderstorms do generate electromagnetic energy, this energy is broadband noise, not a coherent voice broadcast. The storm is irrelevant to the radio's operation (except as a source of static interference).
- **The darkness and power outage** set the emotional stage (isolation, helplessness) but are not physically relevant. The crystal radio works identically in daylight with power available -- it simply does not need power.
- **The age of the device ("1942")** could lead models to suspect it is a novelty or toy rather than a functional receiver. In fact, 1942-era crystal radios were well-engineered and highly functional.
- **The cat's whisker** is an unfamiliar component to most modern people. It looks fragile and primitive. Models might dismiss it as decorative or non-functional. It is the core rectifying element of the radio.

## Design Notes

This scenario tests whether a model can explain a "Ghost Machine" -- a device that appears to violate thermodynamics (producing sound from nothing) but actually has a well-understood physical explanation. The crystal radio is the simplest possible example: it works, it has worked for over a century, and its physics is undergraduate-level electromagnetics.

The scenario is a knowledge test more than a creativity test. It requires the model to either know about crystal radios or to reason from first principles about electromagnetic energy transfer. A model with broad technical knowledge should identify this immediately. A model without radio engineering knowledge but with strong physics reasoning should be able to deduce the answer from the principle that electromagnetic waves carry energy.
